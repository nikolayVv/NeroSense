# NeroSense — Market Analysis

**Prepared:** April 2026 · **Scope:** Total Market, Serviceable Market, Addressable Market, Competitor landscape

---

## 1. What we are sizing

NeroSense is a remote-sensing-first water intelligence platform: Copernicus Sentinel-2/3 satellite ingestion → bloom and water-quality models → autonomous surface robot dispatched to satellite-flagged hotspots → unified report. The use case is phytoplankton/cyanobacteria monitoring on inland reservoirs and rivers, sold initially in Bulgaria.

Market sizing must therefore reflect the intersection of three industries — not any one of them in isolation:

1. **Water quality monitoring** (the broader instrument/sensor/services industry)
2. **Earth observation services for inland water** (the satellite-derived information layer)
3. **WFD-driven public procurement** (the regulatory revenue mechanism in the EU)

This produces a layered TM → SAM → TAM funnel, with each layer narrowing the prior.

## 2. Total Market (TM)

The Total Market is the global water quality monitoring industry — every dollar spent worldwide on measuring water quality, regardless of method (sensor, lab, satellite, manual sampling).

|---|---|---|---|
| Straits Research | USD 5.89 B (2025) | USD 10.88 B by 2034 | 7.6% |
| Stalwart Research Insights (Apr 2026) | USD 6.08 B (2025) | USD 12.18 B by 2035 | 7.2% |
| Emergen Research | USD 4.2 B (2024) | USD 8.5 B by 2034 | 7.3% |
| Verified Market Research | USD 6.18 B (2024) | USD 9.83 B by 2031 | 6.6% |
| Verified Market Reports | USD 3.5 B (2024) | USD 6.8 B by 2033 | 7.8% |

**Convergence point:** Global water quality monitoring market ≈ **USD 6 B in 2026, ≈ USD 10–12 B by 2034**, growing at **~7% CAGR**.

**Composition:** Sensors dominate (~40–50% of revenue); software & services (the layer NeroSense plays in) is the fastest-growing slice. Utility end-users hold ~40% of the market; government/regulatory ~30%; industrial ~25%.

**Why this is the TM, not the SAM:** the bulk of this spend is on physical sensors (pH, turbidity, dissolved oxygen, conductivity probes) sold by Xylem, Hach (Danaher), Endress+Hauser, ABB, Emerson, Horiba, Thermo Fisher. NeroSense does not compete with them — it complements their stack with EO + autonomous validation. They are partners and integrators, not competitors.

## 3. Serviceable Market (SAM)

The Serviceable Market is the slice of the TM that NeroSense can technically serve with its current product: **EO-derived water quality services for inland surface waters in the EU/EEA, plus the autonomous in-situ validation layer that goes with them.**

This is a much smaller and more specific number. Three independent paths converge on roughly the same answer.

### 3.1 Path A — EU regulated water-monitoring spend, EO-eligible slice

- The EU has **~100,000 surface water bodies** monitored under the Water Framework Directive 2000/60/EC.
- Only **37%** of EU surface water bodies achieve "good ecological status" — the gap is the procurement driver.
- Member States are forced into a 6-year RBMP cycle; the 4th cycle (2028–2033) is being drafted now, opening the procurement window for new methodologies.
- EU EEA + JRC water-EO procurement is estimated at **€20–40 M / year** (framework contracts for inland water EO services, mostly captured by EOMAP, CLS, GMV, Brockmann Consult).
- National-level WFD monitoring spend across MS for biological elements (phytoplankton, macrophytes, benthic invertebrates) — the slice EO can actually replace — is conservatively **€150–250 M / year EU-wide** (extrapolating from BG figures: €8–15 M per 6-year cycle for one MS × 27 MS, weighted by water-body count).

### 3.2 Path B — European satellite EO services for environmental applications

- Europe satellite-based EO market: **USD ~1.67 B growth 2023–2028, ~10.4% CAGR** (Technavio).
- Inland-water + coastal water is a thematic sub-segment of Copernicus Land + Marine services.
- The Copernicus 2021–2027 envelope is **€5.4 B** total; the addressable downstream water-quality slice is roughly 3–5% of that = **€160–270 M over the period**, or **€25–40 M / year**, consistent with Path A.

### 3.3 Path C — European water quality monitoring market, services-only

- Europe water quality monitoring market: **USD 2.1 B by 2030** (Grand View Research) at 6.5% CAGR.
- Software & services component within Europe: ~15–20% of that, ≈ **€300–400 M / year**.
- Of which the EO + autonomous in-situ subset is a single-digit percentage (the technology is still in early adoption phase) ≈ **€30–60 M / year**.

### 3.4 SAM consolidation

Triangulating the three paths:

> **NeroSense SAM = €40–80 M / year in 2026, growing toward €100–150 M / year by 2030**, driven by the WFD 4th RBMP cycle, the recast Drinking Water Directive, the Nature Restoration Law, and the upcoming Sentinel-2 Next Generation (S2NG) mission's enhanced inland-water bands (5–10 m resolution, 2–3 day revisit at mid-latitudes).

## 4. Addressable Market (TAM — what NeroSense can realistically capture)

The TAM is the share of the SAM that NeroSense can win in the next 3–5 years given its team, capital, network, and procurement reach. From the project's existing segment analysis, the three sequenced segments roll up cleanly:

### 4.1 Bulgaria — beachhead

| Segment | Buyer set | Annual addressable value |
|---|---|---|
| **Water utilities** | Sofiyska Voda + ~50 ВиК regional operators (EWRC-regulated, ~€700 M sector revenue) | **€7–14 M / year** (source-water monitoring slice, ~1–2% of utility OPEX) |
| **Basin Directorates** | 4 BDs (Danube/Pleven, Black Sea/Varna, East Aegean/Plovdiv, West Aegean/Blagoevgrad), 100% BG territory, ~700 surface water bodies | **€8–15 M / 6-year RBMP cycle** = €1.3–2.5 M / year |
| **National agencies** | MOEW, ExEA (ИАОС) | Bundled inside BD spend; methodology-pilot scope |

**Bulgaria addressable value: ~€10–17 M / year.**

### 4.2 Adjacent EU expansion (post-Bulgaria reference)

The same WFD framework, the same Basin Directorate analogue, the same EU Cohesion Fund mechanism applies in:

- **Romania** — Apele Române, 11 basin administrations, similar infringement pressure on 3rd RBMP delivery
- **Greece** — Special Secretariat for Water, 14 RBDs
- **Croatia, Slovenia, Hungary** — Danube basin co-riparians
- **Italy, Spain, Portugal** — Mediterranean reservoirs with strong cyanobacteria pressure

Veolia Group rollout (Paris HQ) is a parallel B2B vector — Veolia operates utilities across France, Germany, Czechia, Romania, etc. A Sofiyska Voda reference deployment becomes a Veolia Group-wide template.

**Adjacent EU addressable value: €30–60 M / year by 2029** (assuming 2–3 country expansions and one Veolia framework agreement).

### 4.3 EU Executive Agency tier (long horizon)

EEA (Copenhagen), JRC (Ispra), DG ENV, EUMETSAT framework contracts. Largest TAM but EOMAP, Brockmann Consult, CLS, and GMV currently own the channel. Realistic NeroSense entry as a sub-contractor to an EU prime in 2027–2028; lead bid possible 2029.

**Executive tier addressable value: €5–15 M / year for a sub-contractor position; €20–40 M / year for a prime, but multi-year horizon.**

### 4.4 TAM consolidation

> **NeroSense realistic 5-year TAM = €40–90 M / year by 2030**, dependent on (a) successful Iskar pilot, (b) at least one Basin Directorate framework win, (c) EU sub-contractor positioning. This is roughly half of the SAM, which is the right ratio for a focused early-stage entrant in a regulated market with established incumbents.

## 5. Competitor Landscape

The competitor map has four bands, because NeroSense's differentiator is the **EO + autonomous in-situ closed loop** — and no competitor today owns both bands.

### 5.1 Band 1 — Satellite EO water-quality service providers (NeroSense's primary peers)

These are the companies that deliver the same kind of remote-sensing layer NeroSense delivers. They are the competition for the satellite half of NeroSense's stack.

**EOMAP (Germany)** — Founded as an ESA BIC alumnus in Bavaria. Operates the eoapp AQUA platform and built the UNESCO IIWQ World Water Quality Portal. Provides chlorophyll, turbidity, HAB indicators, Secchi depth, total suspended matter from Sentinel-2/3, Landsat, Worldview, Pleiades. Won the Copernicus Award (2011, 2013), Bavarian Innovation Prize 2023, Copernicus Tech Award 2023. Distribution partnerships with Xylem and Fugro. **The dominant EU player. The one NeroSense will see in every tender.**

**Brockmann Consult (Germany)** — Spin-off rooted in ESA's Sentinel-3 OLCI ground segment. Heavy involvement in EU Horizon CyanoLakes project, EOMORES, and the Copernicus C3S/CMEMS validation chain. Strong in algorithmic depth and scientific credibility; less commercial than EOMAP.

**CLS – Collecte Localisation Satellites (France)** — Subsidiary of CNES. Operates ocean, inland water, and environmental EO services across the Copernicus chain. Deep ties to French government and ESA framework contracts. Will appear on EEA tenders.

**GMV (Spain)** — Engineering group with a substantial EO services arm. Frequent prime/sub partner on ESA and EEA framework contracts. Strong in Iberian peninsula and Latin America.

**Sinergise (Slovenia, now part of Planet Labs)** — Operates Sentinel Hub. Originally an EO data-access platform; increasingly offering domain services including water. The infrastructure layer behind many EO startups, including potentially NeroSense.

**CyanoLakes (South Africa)** — Mark Matthews, 2014 Copernicus Masters Ideas Challenge winner. Specialized in cyanobacteria detection from Sentinel-3 OLCI. Deployed in South Africa's Department of Water and Sanitation national database. The most direct functional analogue to the bloom-detection wedge — but no European market presence.

**Gybe (US)** — Newer entrant; combines satellite EO with hyperspectral in-situ buoys. Closer in conceptual model to NeroSense, but US-focused and primarily aimed at industrial customers, not WFD compliance.

**RESAC (Bulgaria)** — Local precedent. Won the Danube Basin Directorate flood-EO tender. Not a direct functional competitor (different domain — floods, not water quality), but the **procurement precedent**: they proved BG basin authorities can buy satellite-derived services via ZOP tender.

### 5.2 Band 2 — In-situ technology providers (the buoy, robot, and ultrasonic side)

These are competitors only for the in-situ half. In practice they're **integration partners or incumbents to coexist with**, not displacers.

**LG Sonic (Netherlands)** — Ultrasonic algae-control buoys. Already deployed at Sofiyska Voda via the EU aqua3S project. Incumbent at the lighthouse account. NeroSense extends what LG Sonic does at a single point to the whole reservoir — they coexist.

**YSI / Xylem (US)** — Multiparameter sondes and buoys. The default in-situ instrument across most utilities globally. Distribution partner for EOMAP's eoapp AQUA. Not a competitor for NeroSense's robot — a complement.

**Hach (Danaher), Endress+Hauser, In-Situ Inc., OTT HydroMet** — Sensor manufacturers, lab analyzers, and SCADA integrations. Same logic: the underlying instrumentation NeroSense's platform aggregates and validates against, not the layer NeroSense sells.

**Saildrone, Liquid Robotics (Boeing), AutoNaut, Ocean Aero, Maritime Robotics, Open Ocean Robotics** — Autonomous Surface Vehicle (USV) manufacturers. Almost entirely focused on **ocean and defense** missions — bathymetry, ISR, fisheries, anti-submarine. **None target inland reservoirs or WFD compliance.** Saildrone alone has raised $325 M over 5 rounds (latest May 2025). Their boats are 7–22 m, ocean-class, completely overscaled for a 30 km² reservoir. NeroSense's robot category — small, electric, reservoir-scale, paired with EO — has no direct vendor competitor in Europe today. This is a genuine open lane.

### 5.3 Band 3 — Local consultancies and lab-based incumbents

**NIMH (Bulgarian National Institute of Meteorology and Hydrology)** — Government scientific institute. Likely partner, possibly competitor on methodology contracts. NeroSense's strategy treats them as a consortium-prime option.

**Sofia University "St. Kliment Ohridski"** — Co-author of the 2023 Iskar phytoplankton risk study. Strategic anchor partner; not a competitor.

**Eko Plus, Balkanika Energy, and other BG environmental consultancies** — Win ZOP tenders with manual sampling + lab analysis methodology. Cheaper on paper, slower in practice, no continuous coverage. NeroSense displaces ~80% of their boat-day OPEX with EO + targeted sampling.

### 5.4 Band 4 — Generic water-quality SaaS dashboards

**52impact (Netherlands), Utilis (Israel), Aquarius / KISTERS, Bentley OpenFlows** — Various data platforms for utility operations. Adjacent, but most don't ingest EO or operate hardware. They sell dashboards; NeroSense sells defensible WFD evidence with an autonomous validation layer.

### 5.5 Competitive positioning summary

| Capability | NeroSense | EOMAP | Brockmann | CLS / GMV | LG Sonic | Saildrone |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sentinel-2/3 inland water analytics | Yes | Yes (leader) | Yes | Yes | No | No |

| Autonomous in-situ validation robot | Yes | No | No | No | Static buoy | Ocean only |
| Closed-loop EO → robot dispatch | Yes (differentiator) | No | No | No | No | No |

The differentiator is line 4. **No European competitor today operationalizes a satellite alert into an autonomous in-situ validation sample within the same product.** That is the moat — and it is the strategic asset the segment analysis already identifies as the X-factor.

## 6. Synthesis — what the numbers say

- **TM is large but mostly irrelevant.** The USD 6 B global water-quality monitoring market is dominated by hardware players NeroSense doesn't compete with. Citing TM is a courtesy figure for investors; it is not the operating market.
- **SAM is the right sizing frame.** €40–80 M / year for EO + autonomous in-situ services in the EU water sector is large enough to build a meaningful business and small enough that EOMAP's dominance has not yet hardened across all segments.
- **TAM is sequenced, not simultaneous.** The €40–90 M / year 5-year capture target only materializes if the segment sequence (BG utility → BG basin directorates → EU agencies) is honored. Skipping segments forfeits the references that gate the next one.
- **Competition is asymmetric.** EOMAP wins on EO maturity and reference customers. NeroSense wins on the closed-loop robot integration and the Bulgaria-led consortium positioning for the 4th RBMP cycle. The two competitive axes don't overlap, which means NeroSense is not displacing EOMAP — it is opening an adjacent category.
- **The procurement window is now.** The 4th RBMP methodology is being drafted in 2026–2027. A Sofia University co-authored methodology referenced in that document — even informally — locks in 6 years of procurement across all four BG basins, with an EU template that travels.
